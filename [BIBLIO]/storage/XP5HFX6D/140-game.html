Award-winning game 140 tests player's rhythmic ability

By Philippa Warr
02 April 13
Leave a comment

Side-scrolling platformer 140 hinges gameplay around the soundtrack for a synaesthetic gaming experience.

The game is a spare time project by Jeppe Carlsen, developer at Playdead and creator of the puzzles for the critically acclaimed Limbo. It takes the form of a minimalist platformer with a colourful abstract art style (a world apart from Limbo) which demands rhythmic ability for success.

As the player navigates the world, the beats from the "energetic, yet melancholic" electronic soundtrack alter the landscape while movement needs to sync with the musical rhythm in order to avoid obstacles.

"The challenge lies in syncing up your moves and jumps to the music-controlled elements," Carlsen told Eurogamer. "The soundtrack evolves in each level as you progress, introducing new elements for you to tackle, and ultimately leads to intense rhythm-based boss fights."

Perhaps not surprisingly, given the way the sound influences both the gameworld and game play 140 was given the Excellence in Audio award at the Independent Games Festival as part of GDC.

140 is expected to be released later in 2013 on PC and Mac.
